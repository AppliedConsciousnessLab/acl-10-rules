Meaning cannot be accessed through confusion.

When perception is distorted by stress, fear, or overload, the mind invents meaning to fill the gap.
That meaning is usually inaccurate, self-blaming, or catastrophic.

Clarity is the prerequisite for meaning.
Not purpose. Not values. Not interpretation.
Clarity.

Clarity means seeing what is actually happening without amplification or distortion.
It requires a regulated nervous system, adequate capacity, and sufficient safety.

When clarity is low:
- Meaning becomes rigid
- Stories replace facts
- Identity collapses into narratives
- Decisions are reactive

When clarity is present:
- Meaning emerges organically
- Perspective widens
- Choice becomes possible
- Insight replaces rumination

You do not find meaning by searching harder.
You find meaning by seeing more clearly.

This rule follows naturally:
Care regulates the system.
Safety enables learning.
Capacity sustains effort.
Clarity allows truth.

Only then does meaning have something real to attach to.

Seek clarity first.
Meaning will reveal itself.
